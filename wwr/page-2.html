Colorado River, United States

The amazing thing about the Colorado river is that it runs through multiple states in the western United States, Utah, California, Arizona, Nevada and Colorado. The rapids along the Colorado River in the Grand Canyon are classified on a scale from I to V, making it suitable for both novice and experienced rafters. Grand Canyon is a wonderful place with a relaxing section, but also breathtaking because of the towering cliffs.

Rafting the Colorado River in the Grand Canyon typically involves multi-day trips, as the river stretches for approximately 277 miles (446 kilometres) within the canyon. These trips can from several days to a few weeks, giving participants a lot of time to enjoy themselves in the canyon's beauty and experience the joy of rafting along every point of the river.

Rafting through the Grand Canyon You'll see beautiful red rock cliffs, beaches, waterfalls and the greatness of the canyon itself. The location of this place makes it a perfect place for water rafting with the inspiring environment.

Note it's essential to obtain a permit. The National Park Service manages the permit system, there is a limited number of permits yearly.

Taking part in a guided rafting trip is a common thing for most visitors, these trips typically include professional guides, and necessary equipment (rafts, life jackets, helmets, etc.).
If you have experience and the needful equipment, you can embark on self-guided rafting trips. This option requires thorough planning, and obtaining permits.
